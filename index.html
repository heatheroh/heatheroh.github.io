Details for our 1st grade POD

General Information

  • We are setting up a learning and activities pod for our 1st graders. We are aiming for a maximum of 5 kids in the pod. Each family would host the pod for a day (or a half day) and take the children through assignments in an encouraging and fun manner while mixing in play and activities. If the hosting family want to hire someone to teach on their hosting day, that is fine

  • FCPS will be fully online this Fall, and while we would want to remain compliant with FCPS requriements, we do not plan on making the virtual classroom the centerpiece for the pod's learning, as our kids will learn better with face-to-face engagement; also, retaining 1st grader's attention in an online setting is obviously challening for any more than ~20mins

  • There are lots of free online worksheets, assigments, and creative activities that we all can use to help our pod learn and have fun

  • It is important that each hosting family abides by good safety practices in terms of COVID19, and conducts temperature checks on kids when they arrive followed by thorough hand-washing or sanitizing

COVID19 guidelines and precautions

  • During this pandemic we have to ensure that we abide by social distancing, mask wearing, and regular hand-washing in order to stay healthy as well as ensuring the safety of everyone in our house. There is currently no vaccine to prevent COVID-19. The best way to prevent illness is to avoid being exposed to this virus. The virus is spread mainly from person-to-person who are in close contact with one another (within about 6 feet) through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs, sneezes or talks. These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby or possibly be inhaled into the lungs. COVID-19 can be spread by people who are not showing symptoms and so do not assume that your friends of people that you meet as not infected.
